The Night before CX-MAS is an annual fundraising event put on by Southern Wonders that takes place the ~night before cx-mas~. This year, it will be a poker ride to benefit Raleigh Community Kickstand.

What's a poker ride? For starters, it's not a race! It's more of a scavenger hunt. You will be given a manifest with clues to locations. Once you figure out the clues, you will then chart a route from there to visit as many locations as you think you can before the deadline. At each stop, you will draw one card from a deck. Your goal will be to collect at least 5 cards for a full hand. At the deadline, we will play a single round of poker and the winner will be the person with the best hand.

Manifest

Use the clues below to guess the establishments. If you've guessed correctly, you'll get the checkpoint address and where to find the volunteer with the cards. The manifest will be unlocked at the start.

Poker Hands

  1. Five of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank (e.g., 9, 9, 9, 9, 2).
  2. Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit.
  3. Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit (e.g., 8, 9, 10, J, Q of hearts).
  4. Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank (e.g., 9, 9, 9, 9, 2).
  5. Full House: Three of a kind combined with a pair (e.g., 7, 7, 7, 4, 4).
  6. Flush: Five cards of the same suit, not in sequence (e.g., 2, 5, 8, J, K of spades).
  7. Straight: Five consecutive cards of different suits (e.g., 4, 5, 6, 7, 8).
  8. Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank (e.g., Q, Q, Q, 5, 9).
  9. Two Pair: Two different pairs (e.g., 10, 10, 4, 4, 6).
  10. One Pair: Two cards of the same rank (e.g., A, A, 3, 8, J).
  11. High Card: When no other hand is made, the highest card wins (e.g., Ace high).

Tiebreaker

If two or more players have the same winning type of hand, the player with the highest-ranking card in their hand wins. If those are tied, the next highest card (kicker) is compared, continuing until a winner is determined. If they are somehow completely tied, the winner will be whoever can guess the number of cable ends in a container.
